Ten Steps Start of Term Checklist

Start of term is here.

This page covers everything you need to get your course ready.

You may not need to complete every item on this list.

1 Set Up or Copy Your Course

If this is your first course ever, you will need to get it started. Copy your course from the previous or master course. To copy a course you can either:

  • Watch a 2-minute video: http://d2l.iddresources.org/documents/video/44
  • Request a copy be made via the D2L Request Forms: http://www.itd.depaul.edu/lmsforms/allforms.aspx

2 Set up COL Widget

To make the COL widget active, please follow these instructions:

  1. Access your course
  2. Click EDIT COURSE in the navigation bar
  3. Click HOMEPAGES
  4. Click SET next to the "CDM Homepage"
  5. Click SET ACTIVE
  6. You're all done!

3 Update Dates

Ensure all date restrictions and updates are correct in the following areas:

  • Content
  • Checklist
  • Discussion Board
  • Dropbox
  • News postings

4 Update e-Reserve & COLTube Links

Ensure that e-Reserve (ARES) and COLTube links work.

COLTube Videos

Help with COLTube is available here.

e-Reserves / ARES

Adding the link to your ARES list is relatively simple and will continue to work after content is copied to your future course. To make the link:

  1. In the edit mode for a D2L page, place your cursor in the spot where you’d like to add the link.
  2. Click on the “Insert Quicklink” icon
  3. In the “Category” dropdown menu, select “External Learning Tools”
  4. In the “External Learning Tools” dropdown menu, select “ARES”
  5. In the “Link Caption” textbox, Type in the hotlink text (for example: “E-reserves for LSP 111”)
  6. Select the button next to “New Window” (we recommend this so students can look back and forth between the reading list in D2L and the items in -reserve list)
  7. Click on “Insert”

If you have a long list of e-reserves: To make finding a specific item easier for your students, add “tags” to each item in the list (for example: all the readings for week 1 are tagged "week 1"; all the week 2 items are tagged "week 2" etc.) so that students can click on a tag and view only the items with that tag.

5 Refresh Syllabus

Ensure your PDF syllabus is up to date and uploaded.

6 Be The Student

Go to the course home and view the course as a student. Check for any content that should not be visible to the students immediately.

7 Open Up

Make your course active for students. To make your course available:

  1. Access your course
  2. Click EDIT COURSE
  3. Click COURSE OFFERING INFORMATION
  4. Check the checkbox next to "Course is Active."
  5. Click SAVE and your students have access.

8 Welcome Email

Send your students a welcome email inviting them to access the course, ideally including the following:

  • Introduce yourself
  • Provide contact information for yourself including preferred method of contact (email/phone) and standard response time
  • Provide information about when the course will officially begin
  • Provide schedule information (what day will each week/module begin end)
  • Set expectations for "attending" class (how much time each week they should typically expect to spend in the course site)
  • Direct students to important parts of the syllabus
  • Direct students to the help sections of the class

9 Post The News

Post the welcome email as a news announcement. Not everyone checks email (especially students).
