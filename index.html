Welcome DePaul CDM faculty!

The CDM Desire2Learn Template provides a pre-packaged 10-week course framework, D2L documentation, and layout CSS / JS in a zip file.

Simply download the zip file below, access your course in D2L, upload the zip file, and start using the template.

What's Included in the Template?

Instructor Only Files Cannot be seen by students.

These files (Fig. 1 in Red) contain information useful to you when building and running your course. There are four files here, the first of which, Using The CDM Template, you are reading now. The included files are:

  • Using The CDM Template explains what is included in this template.
  • A Quick D2L Overview describes the absolute basics of D2L's features.
  • Working with Foundation explains how to use Foundation, and provides example layouts.
  • Start of Term Checklist explains how to get your D2L course rolling.
  • Course Developer and Resources explains how to get in touch with me, what I can help with, and how to submit feature requests.

Sample Module is an example module where you can experiment and also includes notes describing the type of content intended for each section and it's pedagogical value.

Getting Started Module Visible to students.

This module (Fig. 1 in Green) includes information to help your students get started in the course. Providing a quick introduction and explaining what is expected can go a long way toward keeping student confusion (and therefore, email) to a minimum.

Modules 1-10 Visible to students.

There are 10 modules here (Fig. 1 in Blue) by default. Remove modules and sub-sections you don't want, add some that you do want, and make the modules your own. I'm just providing a scaffold for you to get started in D2L.

Installing the Template

Here are the instructions (it's mostly clicking next):

  1. Download the template
  2. Log onto D2L at http://d2l.depaul.edu
  3. Click the pencil beside the course where you'll use the template
  4. Click "Import / Export / Copy Compontents" in the left "Course Admin Tools" area
  5. Choose "Import Components"
  6. Click BROWSE and select the template file
  7. Click NEXT and wait a moment
  8. Click NEXT
  9. Check "Select All Components"
  10. Click NEXT
  11. Click NEXT and wait a moment
  12. Click NEXT
  13. Excellent! The template has been installed!

To get started click "Course Home" in the navigation bar and follow the instructions.

Migrate your COL files to D2L in Four Steps

Printable

Step One – Organize Your Files

  1. Download all your course files to your computer.
  2. On your computer, put the files into a folder structure. The folders should be set up in a way that makes the most sense to you and your students. Common ways to organize content are by week, module, or topic.

    Files organized by Week

    Files organized by Module

    Files organized by Topic

  3. Make a ZIP file with all the folders in it. You can usually do this by selecting all the folders, right-clicking, and choosing "Archive" from the menu.

Step Two – Extract the Zip in D2L

  1. Log into D2L at http://d2l.depaul.edu.
  2. Click on your course.
  3. Click (in the nav bar).
  4. Click MANAGE FILES (lower left).
  5. Click UPLOAD.
  6. Click BROWSE and choose your ZIP file.
  7. Click UPLOAD.
  8. Hover your mouse over the ZIP file (see Fig. 4).
  9. Click the down arrow that appears.
  10. Click UNZIP.
  11. You should now see all the folders and files in D2L.

Unzipping in D2L (Steps 8-10)

Step Three – Create the Modules (aka Folders)

You will be creating new D2L Modules that correspond to the folders in your ZIP file. In D2L, folders are called "modules" and files are called "topics."

  1. Click CONTENT
  2. Click NEW MODULE
  3. Name the new module after a folder from your ZIP file
  4. Click SAVE AND NEW
  5. Repeat Steps 2-4 until you have created a module that corresponds to the folders in your ZIP file

Step Four – Create the Topics (aka Links to Files)

  1. Click CONTENT
  2. Click ADD MULTIPLE TOPICS
  3. From the PARENT MODULE dropdown, choose the module you would like to add files to
  4. From the long list of files below, check box next to the corresponding Folder; all the items in that folder are now checked.
  5. The TOPIC TITLE (what students will see) has been auto-generated for each file; update the titles if necessary.
  6. Click ADD

Repeat steps 2-5 for each module/folder

That’s all. You’ve migrated your documents from COL to D2L. To reorder the modules and topics in D2L, click the little green arrows.
