Migrate your COL files to D2L in Four Steps

Step One – Organize Your Files

  1. Download all your course files to your computer.
  2. On your computer, put the files into a folder structure. The folders should be set up in a way that makes the most sense to you and your students. Common ways to organize content are by week, module, or topic.

  3. Make a ZIP file with all the folders in it. You can usually do this by selecting all the folders, right-clicking, and choosing "Archive" from the menu.

Step Two – Extract the Zip in D2L

  1. Log into D2L at http://d2l.depaul.edu.
  2. Click on your course.
  3. Click (in the nav bar).
  4. Click (lower left).
  5. Click UPLOAD.
  6. Click BROWSE and choose your ZIP file.
  7. Click UPLOAD.
  8. Hover your mouse over the ZIP file (see Fig. 4).
  9. Click the down arrow that appears.
  10. Click UNZIP.
  11. You should now see all the folders and files in D2L.

Unzipping in D2L (Steps 8-10)

Step Three – Create the Modules (aka Folders)

You will be creating new D2L Modules that correspond to the folders in your ZIP file. In D2L, folders are called "modules" and files are called "topics."

  1. Click CONTENT
  2. Click NEW MODULE
  3. Name the new module after a folder from your ZIP file
  4. Click SAVE AND NEW
  5. Repeat Steps 2-4 until you have created a module that corresponds to the folders in your ZIP file

Step Four – Create the Topics (aka Links to Files)

  1. Click CONTENT
  2. Click ADD MULTIPLE TOPICS
  3. From the PARENT MODULE dropdown, choose the module you would like to add files to
  4. From the long list of files below, check box next to the corresponding Folder; all the items in that folder are now checked.
  5. The TOPIC TITLE (what students will see) has been auto-generated for each file; update the titles if necessary.
  6. Click ADD

Repeat steps 2-5 for each module/folder

That’s all. You’ve migrated your documents from COL to D2L. To reorder the modules and topics in D2L, click the little green arrows ().
