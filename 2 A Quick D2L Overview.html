Delivering Information to Students

Content

Your students can access your course documents, materials, PDFs, project files, etc through the Content area of D2L.

There are three important areas in Content:

Manage Files
Where PDFs, images, PPT, HTML, and other files live.
Manage Content
Where you create the structure your students will see.
View Content
Where you and the students access course materials.

Classlist

Send email to your students and track their progress in the course.

News Located on the Course Home

Post announcements students will see when accessing your course. If students subscribe (recommended), they will also receive an email.

Assessing Students

The tools below allow you to accept and grade student work. All tools can link to Grades, making grading easier.

Discussions Part 1

Discussions are a space where your students can engage in discussions about:

  • Topics intended to enrich mastry of course material
  • Tasks or topics they're struggling with
  • Group project collaboration

Dropbox

The Dropbox is where your students can submit documents (papers, zip files, etc). The Dropbox allows for plagiairism detection and group submissions.

Quizzes

Quizzes allow you to create banks of questions (Question Libraries) to assess students on their ability to remember and understand information from your course.

Quiz features include:

  • Randomized sub-sets of question library sets
  • Review quizzes build from all course questions
  • Final exams with random selections from all weeks
  • Automatic grading

Making Life Easier

The tools below can make it easier to answer questions, communicate with students, and calculate grades.

Discussions Part 2

Discussions are also a space where your students can ask questions publically. No more answering the same question five times a term via email.

Once term is over, collect the best questions into a ready-made FAQ!

Grades

You can drop the lowest, curve, and auto-import grades from the Dropbox, Quizzes, and Discussions. Set up your grades once and D2L will calculate for you forever.

Administrating Your Course

EDIT COURSE

This is where the bulk of your course administration will happen.

Navigation

Change the links that appear on the navigation bar or change the colors and background image.

Course Offering Information

Make your course available to students.

Groups

Set up, edit, or enroll users in groups; create group work areas.

Homepages

Activate the CDM Homepage (places COL Widget on homepage).

Import / Export / Copy Components

Copy your course from another course or term.

View User Progress

View user activity for individual users enrolled in this Course Offering, including login history, participation in discussions, content access, and grades.
